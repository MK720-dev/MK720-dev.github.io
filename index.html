About Me

Where I'm From

I was born and raised in the Mediterranean, in Tunisia—a country shaped by ancient civilizations, scientific heritage, and a deep cultural respect for learning. Academics were always central in my life. After ranking among the top students nationally in both the elementary and middle school national exams, I earned admission into two of the most selective public institutions in the country: the Collège Pilote les Berges du Lac 2 for middle school and the Pioneer School of Ariana (LPA) for high school, where I completed the mathematics section. These formative years instilled in me discipline, curiosity, and a strong foundation in analytical thinking—values that continue to guide my work in machine learning and research today.

Sports & Personal Interests

Athletics have always played a central role in my life, especially judo. Training taught me discipline, resilience, and the importance of technical precision — qualities that translate directly into my work in machine learning and computational modeling. I’m also deeply interested in ancient history, particularly Carthage and its scientific, military, and cultural legacy. This duality between physical discipline and historical curiosity shapes both how I learn and what I build.

Research Interests & Aspirations

My research interests lie in robust machine learning and optimization, with a focus on understanding how learning systems behave in real-world, high-stakes settings. I am particularly interested in bridging the persistent gap between ML theory—optimization dynamics, stability, generalization— and the practical deployment of models in domains where reliability matters, such as healthcare, rehabilitation, and scientific computing. Long-term, I aim to pursue a PhD and work toward developing learning systems that are not only accurate, but stable, interpretable, and robust under real-world conditions.
