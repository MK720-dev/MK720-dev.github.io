About Me

Where I'm From

I was born and raised in the Mediterranean, in Tunisia—a country shaped by ancient civilizations, scientific heritage, and a deep cultural respect for learning. Academics were always central in my life. After ranking among the top students nationally in both the elementary and middle school national exams, I earned admission into two of the most selective public institutions in the country: the Collège Pilote les Berges du Lac 2 and the Pioneer School of Ariana (LPA), where I completed the mathematics section.

Sports & Personal Interests

Athletics have always played a central role in my life, especially judo. Training taught me discipline, resilience, and the importance of technical precision— qualities that translate directly into my work in machine learning and computational modeling. I’m also deeply interested in ancient history, particularly Carthage.

Research Interests & Aspirations

I work at the intersection of robust machine learning and optimization, with a focus on understanding how learning systems behave in real-world, high-stakes settings. My goal is to help bridge the gap between ML theory and practical deployment in domains where reliability matters.

Research Agenda

  • Optimization-aware tools for understanding neural network dynamics
  • AI and AR systems for physical therapy and movement analysis
  • Finite element modeling of biological tissues
  • Machine learning for biomedical imaging and diagnostics
