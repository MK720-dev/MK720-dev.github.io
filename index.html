About Me

Where I'm From

I was born and raised in the Mediterranean, in Tunisia — a place shaped by ancient civilizations, layered history, and a strong cultural memory. Growing up between the ruins of Carthage and the modern city taught me to appreciate both tradition and innovation. This mix of heritage and curiosity has deeply influenced the way I approach science, technology, and research.

Sports & Personal Interests

Athletics have always played a central role in my life, especially judo. Training taught me discipline, resilience, and the importance of technical precision — qualities that translate directly into my work in machine learning and computational modeling. I’m also deeply interested in ancient history, particularly Carthage and its scientific, military, and cultural legacy. This duality between physical discipline and historical curiosity shapes both how I learn and what I build.

Research Interests & Aspirations

My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, biomedical imaging, and computational modeling . I aim to build systems that combine data-driven intelligence with a physical understanding of the human body. Long-term, I aspire to pursue a PhD and contribute to the development of AI-assisted healthcare tools, optimization-aware neural architectures, and computational models that reveal the mechanical truths behind biological structures and movement.

Research Agenda

  • Optimization-aware tools for understanding neural network training dynamics
  • AI and AR systems that enhance physical therapy through motion analysis and feedback
